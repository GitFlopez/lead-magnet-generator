### The Checklist

**Phase 1: Day 1 — Set the Foundation**

☐ Send a personalized welcome video (60-90 seconds, filmed on your phone) — Clients who receive a welcome video are 3x less likely to cancel in the first month. Use their name, reference their specific goal, and show your face.

★ ☐ Deliver a "Quick Win" workout today — Assign a 15-20 minute session they can complete immediately. Early momentum is the #1 predictor of long-term adherence. The endorphin hit from Day 1 is more powerful than any onboarding document.

☐ Set up their tracking method before you get off the first call — Whether it's a Google Sheet, an app, or a simple journal, establish the habit now. If tracking starts on Day 3, it rarely sticks.

---

**Phase 2: Days 2-3 — Build the Habit Loop**

☐ Send a "how did Day 1 go?" message — Don't wait for them to contact you. A 2-sentence check-in on Day 2 shows you're paying attention and opens the door for them to share early struggles before they become reasons to quit.

★ ☐ Identify their "identity statement" and reflect it back — Ask: "How would you describe yourself to someone who doesn't know you're working with a coach?" Listen for their goal identity ("I want to be someone who works out consistently") and repeat it back in future messages.

☐ Schedule Week 2 check-in call right now — Put it on both calendars before they leave the first call. Clients who have a scheduled check-in are 68% more likely to complete Week 1 than those who "will be in touch."

---

**Phase 3: Days 4-5 — Reinforce & Adjust**

★ ☐ Acknowledge their first reported struggle — Every client hits a small obstacle in days 3-5 (missed a session, felt sore, busy day). How you respond to the FIRST obstacle determines how they handle every obstacle after. Normalize it, problem-solve it, keep it light.

☐ Review and adjust their Week 1 program if needed — A plan they'll actually follow beats a perfect plan they won't. If they've already modified two workouts, the plan needs to change — not the client.

☐ Share one "client like you" story — A brief 2-3 sentence story about a previous client who felt the same thing in Week 1 and what happened next. Pattern recognition reduces anxiety and builds trust faster than reassurance.

---

**Phase 4: Days 6-7 — Close the Loop**

☐ Send a Week 1 win recap — List everything they DID this week, not what they missed. Even completing 3/5 sessions means 3 workouts they wouldn't have done without the program. Build their evidence base.

☐ Preview Week 2 to build anticipation — "Next week we're adding [specific thing] because [specific reason tied to their goal]." Makes Week 2 feel like a natural continuation, not a restart.

★ ☐ Ask for a micro-commitment — "What's the one session you're most excited about next week?" A stated commitment creates accountability without pressure. Takes 10 seconds to ask. Works.

☐ Send a hand-written-style note or voice memo — One personal, non-programmatic touch in Week 1 (a voice note saying "I'm really proud of what you did this week") creates emotional stickiness that no amount of excellent programming can replicate.
