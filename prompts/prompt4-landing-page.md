# Prompt 4: Opt-In Landing Page + Delivery Email

## When to Use
Use this after you've created your lead magnet (Prompts 1, 2, or 3) to build the complete opt-in funnel. This prompt generates the landing page copy that converts visitors into subscribers, plus the delivery email that delivers the promised resource and sets up the relationship.

## The Prompt

```
You are a direct-response copywriter specializing in lead generation landing pages.

Create complete opt-in landing page copy and a delivery email for the following:

LEAD MAGNET TITLE: [INSERT TITLE — e.g., "The 12-Step Email List Checklist for Coaches"]
LEAD MAGNET TYPE: [checklist / ebook / quiz / template / video]
TARGET AUDIENCE: [INSERT AUDIENCE — e.g., "Health coaches with under 500 subscribers"]
PRIMARY PAIN POINT: [INSERT PAIN — e.g., "Don't know how to grow their email list without paid ads"]
PROMISED OUTCOME: [INSERT OUTCOME — e.g., "Get their first 100 subscribers in 30 days using only organic content"]
YOUR NAME/BRAND: [INSERT NAME]
ONE PIECE OF SOCIAL PROOF: [INSERT PROOF — e.g., "Used by 300+ coaches" / "Featured in Forbes" / "As used by my top clients" / "leave blank if none"]

Generate ALL of the following:

**SECTION 1: Headlines (A/B Test Pair)**

Version A — Outcome-focused:
- Headline (under 10 words, promise the result)
- Subheadline (25-35 words: who it's for + what they get + how long it takes)

Version B — Pain-focused:
- Headline (under 10 words, call out the problem)
- Subheadline (25-35 words: acknowledge the struggle + flip it to the solution)

Score each version 1-10 for clarity, specificity, and emotional resonance. Recommend one.

**SECTION 2: Benefit Stack (3 bullets)**
Write 3 benefit bullets in this format:
✓ [Specific outcome] — [brief "so that" explanation in 10-15 words]

Each bullet should answer "what will I be able to DO after getting this?" — not "what does it contain?"

Weak: ✓ 12-step checklist for building your email list
Strong: ✓ Know exactly what to set up in your first 72 hours — so you never waste time on the wrong platform again

**SECTION 3: Social Proof Block**
Using the social proof you provided, write 1-3 of these elements (whichever are applicable):
- Testimonial pull quote (if you have one, format it properly)
- Credibility line ("Join 300+ coaches who've downloaded this guide")
- Authority line ("Used by clients who have grown from 0 to 1,000 subscribers")
- If no social proof provided, write a "who made this" credibility paragraph instead (2-3 sentences)

**SECTION 4: Opt-In Form Copy**
- Form headline (above the fields): one line, urgency or value-based
- Email field label
- Button text (3-5 words, action-oriented: "Send Me the Checklist" not "Submit")
- Privacy micro-copy (under the button, under 15 words)

**SECTION 5: A/B CTA Variants**
Write 3 button text options. Score each:
1. [Option] — [score/10] — [why it works or doesn't]

**SECTION 6: Delivery Email**
Subject line: (3 options — [curious] / [direct] / [personal])

Email body:
- Opening line (warm, personal, acknowledges they just opted in)
- 1-2 sentences confirming what they're about to get
- The "download" or access instruction (clear, specific)
- One tip they can use TODAY (from the lead magnet content) — build immediate value
- Soft CTA: what to do next (reply to this email / follow you somewhere / check out X)
- Sign-off (name + short tagline)

P.S. line: (one sentence that teases the next email OR introduces an upgrade offer)

Format everything cleanly. Use markdown headers. Make all copy immediately ready to paste into a landing page builder.
```

## Expected Output

- 2 headline/subheadline pairs (A/B test) with recommendation
- 3 benefit bullets
- Social proof block
- Opt-in form copy (headline + labels + button + privacy)
- 3 CTA button options with scoring
- Delivery email (3 subject line options + full body + P.S.)

## Tips

- **The button text is the highest-leverage A/B test.** "Get the Free Checklist" vs "Send Me the Checklist" vs "Yes! I Want the Checklist" — each converts differently by audience. Run all three if your platform allows.
- **The delivery email is your first real impression.** Don't just send a link. Give them one tip they can use today, before they even open the PDF.
- **The P.S. is the most-read line in any email.** Use it to tease your next email or make a soft offer — don't waste it on "thanks again."
