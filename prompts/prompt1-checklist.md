# Prompt 1: Checklist & Quick-Win Guide

## When to Use
Use this when your audience needs a step-by-step action list they can complete quickly. Checklists have the highest opt-in conversion rates of any lead magnet format because the perceived effort is low and the value is immediate.

Best for: process-oriented topics, "how to" tasks, audit-style content.

## The Prompt

```
You are an expert content marketer creating a high-converting lead magnet checklist.

Create a complete checklist lead magnet for the following:

TOPIC: [INSERT TOPIC — e.g., "Email list building for coaches"]
TARGET AUDIENCE: [INSERT AUDIENCE — e.g., "Health coaches with under 500 subscribers"]
PRIMARY PAIN POINT: [INSERT PAIN — e.g., "Don't know where to start or what to create"]
DESIRED OUTCOME: [INSERT OUTCOME — e.g., "Get their first 100 subscribers in 30 days"]

Generate ALL of the following:

**SECTION 1: Lead Magnet Title**
- Write 5 title options using these formulas:
  - "The [Number]-Step [Outcome] Checklist"
  - "[Number] Things You Need to [Outcome] Before [Deadline]"
  - "The [Audience] [Outcome] Checklist: [Number] Action Items"
  - "[Outcome] in [Timeframe]: The Step-by-Step Checklist"
  - "The Ultimate [Topic] Checklist for [Audience]"
- Score each title 1-10 for opt-in appeal (specificity, urgency, clarity)
- Recommend the best title with rationale

**SECTION 2: The Checklist (PDF-Ready Format)**
Create 12-15 action items organized into 3-4 logical sections. For each section:
- Section header (e.g., "Phase 1: Foundation", "Week 1 Actions")
- 3-4 checklist items per section
- Each item: ☐ [Action verb] + [specific action] + [why it matters in 1 sentence]
- Mark 2-3 items as ★ QUICK WIN (completable in under 10 minutes)

Example format:
**Phase 1: Set Up Your Foundation (Days 1-3)**
☐ Choose your email platform — Select ConvertKit, MailerLite, or Klaviyo based on your list size (free tiers available up to 500-1,000 subscribers)
★ ☐ Write your welcome email — Draft a 3-paragraph email that delivers the lead magnet, sets expectations, and asks one question to start a conversation
☐ Create a simple opt-in form — Use your platform's built-in form builder; keep it to first name + email only (every extra field drops conversion by 11%)

**SECTION 3: Landing Page Teaser (50-75 words)**
Write a teaser paragraph for the opt-in page that:
- Opens with the pain point
- Promises the outcome
- Names 2-3 specific items in the checklist
- Ends with a soft CTA ("Download the free checklist →")

**SECTION 4: Introduction Text (for inside the PDF)**
Write a 100-word introduction for the beginning of the checklist that:
- Acknowledges the reader's current struggle
- Promises what they'll be able to do after completing the checklist
- Gives them permission to use it their own way (skip steps, start anywhere)
- Ends with an encouraging line

Format the full output as clean, copy-paste-ready text. Use markdown headers for sections.
```

## Expected Output

- 5 title options with scores and recommendation
- 12-15 checklist items in 3-4 sections (PDF-ready)
- 50-75 word landing page teaser
- 100-word PDF introduction

## Tips

- **Pick a specific outcome.** "Get more email subscribers" is too broad. "Get your first 100 subscribers in 30 days using only organic content" is specific enough to create a high-value checklist.
- **Quick Wins matter.** The ★ items create early momentum. Put at least one in Section 1.
- **Length sweet spot:** 12-15 items. Under 10 feels thin; over 20 feels overwhelming.
