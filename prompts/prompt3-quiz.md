# Prompt 3: Quiz Lead Magnet

## When to Use
Use this when you want to segment your list from the moment of opt-in. Quizzes get 2-3x higher opt-in rates than static lead magnets because they're interactive — the reader is invested before they give you their email. They also segment your list automatically, so your follow-up sequence can be personalized from day one.

Best for: audiences with distinct sub-groups, coaches targeting different experience levels, tools/products with multiple use cases.

## The Prompt

```
You are an expert quiz funnel designer creating a high-converting lead magnet quiz.

Create a complete quiz lead magnet for the following:

TOPIC: [INSERT TOPIC — e.g., "Finding the right business model for your skills"]
TARGET AUDIENCE: [INSERT AUDIENCE — e.g., "Aspiring online entrepreneurs who haven't launched yet"]
PRIMARY PAIN POINT: [INSERT PAIN — e.g., "Don't know which type of business to start, overwhelmed by options"]
3 RESULT SEGMENTS: [INSERT SEGMENTS — e.g., "The Service Business, The Product Creator, The Community Builder" — or write "generate for me"]
YOUR NAME/BRAND: [INSERT NAME]

Generate ALL of the following:

**SECTION 1: Quiz Title**
Write 5 quiz title options using high-conversion formats:
- "What Type of [X] Are You?" (personalization format)
- "Which [X] Model Is Right for You?" (decision format)
- "Are You Ready to [Outcome]? Find Out in 2 Minutes" (readiness format)
- "What's Your [X] Personality?" (identity format)
- "Which [X] Path Will Get You to [Outcome] Fastest?" (speed format)
Score each 1-10. Recommend the best.

**SECTION 2: Quiz Intro Copy (50-75 words)**
Write landing page intro copy that appears before the quiz starts:
- One sentence that captures the pain
- One sentence that promises the value of the result
- "Takes 2 minutes. No email required until the end." (reduce friction)
- CTA button text: "Start the Quiz →"

**SECTION 3: The Quiz (5 Questions)**
For each question:
- Question text (clear, conversational, not jargon)
- 3-4 answer options (A through D)
- Which result segment each answer points to (in brackets, not visible to user)
- Scoring guide: each answer = 1 point toward a segment

Design the questions so they reveal personality/preference, not knowledge. No right or wrong answers.

Example:
**Question 3: When you think about building a business, which feels most natural to you?**
A) Solving a specific problem for a specific person one-on-one [Service]
B) Creating something once that helps thousands of people [Product]
C) Building a space where people support each other [Community]
D) I'm honestly not sure — I like all of these [Balanced → default to Product]

**SECTION 4: Email Capture Gate**
Write the copy for the email capture screen (appears after question 5, before results):
- Headline: "Your results are ready!"
- One sentence about what the result will show them
- Email field label + button text
- Privacy micro-copy: "No spam. Unsubscribe anytime."

**SECTION 5: Results Pages (one per segment)**
For each of the 3 result segments, write:
- Result headline: "You're The [Segment Name]!" 
- 3-sentence description of this type (validating, specific, aspirational)
- 3 specific strengths of this type
- 1 common challenge this type faces
- Personalized next step recommendation (specific to their segment)
- CTA: "Your free [resource] for [Segment Name]s →" (personalized)

**SECTION 6: Thank-You / Delivery Email**
Write the email sent after opt-in:
- Subject line: "[First Name], here are your results: You're The [Segment]!"
- Body: restate the result, give 1 personalized tip, deliver or link the lead magnet, soft CTA
- P.S. line: segmentation hook ("P.S. I send weekly [topic] tips specifically for [Segment Name]s — keep an eye out")

Format everything cleanly. Use markdown headers for each section.
```

## Expected Output

- 5 title options with recommendation
- Quiz intro copy
- 5 questions with 3-4 answers each and scoring logic
- Email capture gate copy
- 3 results pages (full copy for each segment)
- Delivery email

## Tips

- **Segments should feel like identities, not just categories.** "The Strategic Builder" is better than "Type B." People share results that make them feel seen.
- **Keep questions to exactly 5.** Under 5 feels too quick to be meaningful. Over 7 is too long; people drop off.
- **The email capture gate converts best with "results are ready" framing** — the reader is invested and just wants to see their answer.
