# Prompt 2: Ebook / Mini-Guide Outline

## When to Use
Use this when your audience needs depth — a framework, a methodology, or a complete education on a topic. Ebooks position you as the definitive expert and attract higher-intent subscribers (people who are serious about solving the problem, not just browsing).

Best for: complex topics, frameworks, step-by-step systems, "how it works" education.

## The Prompt

```
You are an expert content strategist creating a high-authority lead magnet ebook outline.

Create a complete ebook/mini-guide outline for the following:

TOPIC: [INSERT TOPIC — e.g., "Pricing your freelance services"]
TARGET AUDIENCE: [INSERT AUDIENCE — e.g., "Freelance designers with 1-3 years of experience"]
PRIMARY PAIN POINT: [INSERT PAIN — e.g., "Undercharging, losing clients on price, not knowing what to charge"]
DESIRED OUTCOME: [INSERT OUTCOME — e.g., "Confidently raise rates by 30-50% without losing clients"]
YOUR NAME/BRAND: [INSERT NAME — e.g., "Alex Rivera, Freelance Finance"]

Generate ALL of the following:

**SECTION 1: Title & Subtitle**
Write 3 title + subtitle combinations using these formulas:
- Formula A: "[Outcome] for [Audience]: [Promise or Method]"
- Formula B: "The [Adjective] Guide to [Topic]: [Specific Result in Timeframe]"
- Formula C: "[Number]-[Unit] [Outcome]: [What Makes This Different]"

Score each 1-10 for authority, specificity, and opt-in appeal. Recommend the best.

**SECTION 2: Introduction Hook (200-250 words)**
Write a compelling introduction that:
- Opens with a story or scenario the reader will immediately recognize ("You've been freelancing for two years, and you're still charging what you charged on day one...")
- Names the 3 specific mistakes most people in this situation make
- Promises the outcome this guide delivers
- Establishes your authority (brief, 2 sentences)
- Ends with a "here's what we're going to cover" bridge to the chapters

**SECTION 3: Chapter Outline (5-7 chapters)**
For each chapter:
- Chapter number and title (action-oriented, not descriptive: "Calculate Your True Hourly Rate" not "Pricing Basics")
- 4-5 bullet points of what the chapter covers (these become section headers inside the chapter)
- One "chapter promise" sentence: "By the end of this chapter, you'll be able to [specific action]"
- Estimated reading time

**SECTION 4: Conclusion + CTA**
Write a 100-word conclusion that:
- Summarizes the transformation the reader has just completed
- Gives them one immediate next step (specific action, not vague)
- Transitions to your offer (discovery call / product / next resource) with a non-pushy CTA

**SECTION 5: About the Author Blurb (75-100 words)**
Write an author bio that:
- Establishes credibility with 2-3 specific proof points (years of experience, clients served, results achieved)
- Shows personality (1 sentence about who you are beyond the professional persona)
- Includes a "find me at" line for website/social

Format the full output cleanly. Use markdown headers.
```

## Expected Output

- 3 title+subtitle options with recommendation
- 200-250 word introduction
- 5-7 chapter outlines (title + 4-5 bullets + promise + reading time)
- 100-word conclusion with CTA
- 75-100 word author bio

## Tips

- **Chapter titles should promise outcomes, not describe topics.** "How to Handle Scope Creep" is weak. "The 3-Sentence Script That Stops Scope Creep Before It Starts" is strong.
- **Keep it under 20 pages.** A "mini-guide" (5,000-8,000 words) converts better than a 50-page PDF. People will actually read it.
- **The CTA in the conclusion is your first conversion opportunity.** Make it specific: "Book a free 30-minute pricing audit" > "Visit my website."
